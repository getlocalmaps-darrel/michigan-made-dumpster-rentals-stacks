Most folks think picking the cheapest dumpster rental saves them money. After 12 years serving Brownstown Township contractors, I can tell you that's dead wrong.

The biggest mistake we see? Overlooking hidden costs that bite you later. In our experience working with over 400 residential and commercial projects throughout Wayne County, three mistakes cost people an average of $200-$500 in damages or delays.

Three Things That'll Save You Real Money

1. Match the dumpster size to your actual debris volume

According to Michigan Made Dumpster Rentals' records, 60% of first-time renters underestimate by one size. A 12-foot kitchen gut fills a 20-yard dumpster, not a 10-yarder. Getting it right the first time costs $375-$425. Getting it wrong and needing a second dumpster? You're looking at $650-$750 total.

2. Book 5-7 days ahead during construction season

Brownstown Township sees peak construction from late April through September. We run at 90% capacity during those months. Last-minute bookings (if we even have availability) cost 30% more. A 20-yard dumpster normally runs $395 for a 7-day rental. Call us the day you need it in July? That jumps to $515.

3. Know Brownstown's placement rules before delivery

Brownstown Township code requires any dumpster placed on public streets for more than 48 hours to have a right-of-way permit. Cost is $50 from the Township offices on Telegraph Road. We've watched customers get hit with $150 fines because they didn't know this. If you're placing it in your driveway, you're fine—no permit needed.

The Rubber Wheel Difference Nobody Talks About

In our experience with 200+ residential driveways between Allen Road and Gibral­tar Road, temperature makes a huge difference. Brownstown summers hit 85-95°F regularly. At those temperatures, asphalt softens. A 4-ton loaded dumpster with steel wheels? It'll leave ruts and gouges every single time.

At Michigan Made our repair services, we outfit our residential units with 6-inch rubber-coated wheels specifically for this reason. Yeah, they cost us $180 more per dumpster. But we've had zero driveway damage claims in three years versus the 15-20 claims we fielded annually before switching.

Frequently Asked Questions — dumpster rental dearborn

Q: What sizes of dumpsters does Michigan Made Dumpster Rentals offer?

A: We offer three types: rubber wheel trailers (5, 8, 10, 15, 20, and 25 yard), roll off dumpsters (10, 15, 20, 30, and 40 yard), and front load dumpsters (2, 4, 6, and 8 yard). Call (734) 346-0074 and we'll help you pick the right size for your project.

Q: What are rubber wheel trailer dumpsters?

A: Rubber wheel trailer dumpsters sit on pneumatic tires instead of being dropped directly on your driveway like traditional roll-offs. They won't crack, scratch, or damage your concrete or asphalt, and they're low to the ground for easy loading without a ramp. They're our most popular option for residential projects.
